Introduction to Software Reverse Engineering

We deeply appreciate researchers in the community for their inspiring works, which provide us with rich materials to develop this course and push forward our research in this direction.

This course is designed for both undergraduate students and graduate students at all levels. Please check the prerequisites before registering for this course.

Software reverse engineering is a process or method through which one tries to understand through logical reasoning how a previously made software accomplishes a task with very little insight into exactly how it does so. It is essentially the process of opening up or dissecting a system to see how it works, in order to duplicate or enhance it. Depending on the system under consideration and the technologies employed, the knowledge gained during reverse engineering can help with repurposing obsolete objects, doing security analysis, or learning how something works (see wiki for more background).

This course was developed by Qingkai Shi based on his research and practical experience in the area of reverse engineering. One can refer to his homepage about his research. In this course, we will study recent advances in software reverse engineering as well as how existing techniques are applied to securing software systems. The course covers reverse-engineering and anti-reverse-engineering techniques in a broad range of scenarios, including disassembly, decompiling, reverse engineering network protocols, securing the Internet of Things, and many others.

Contact Qingkai Shi if you find bugs or want to include more materials in the course.

Prerequisites

Before registering for this course, a student needs to check if he has basic knowledge of data structures and algorithms, program analysis and compilers, and fundamental programming languages such as C/C++ and Java. It could be better if a student understands assembly code and networking, but this is not a prerequisite.
